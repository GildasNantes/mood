What is an “emotional weather map”?

We talk about the weather because it’s a simple shared language: today is sunny, cloudy, stormy. Mood2Know uses the same idea for emotions. An emotional weather map is a collective snapshot of how people feel across the world—right now—shown as a map, not as a profile.

Instead of long surveys, Mood2Know asks one question: How is your mood, from 0 to 10? You answer in one click, anonymously, and the map reveals aggregated moods from other participants.

How Mood2Know works

  • Pick a mood (0 to 10).
  • Locate + Save (your device provides a location).
  • See the world’s mood on a live map with clustered markers.

Mood2Know is designed to be lightweight: no account, no feed, no social pressure—just participation.

Anonymous by design

Mood2Know is not a mood diary. It is not therapy, and it does not try to explain why you feel a certain way. The goal is to make a collective signal visible without collecting personal stories.

The system stores a simple data point: mood + approximate location + a random anonymous identifier. That’s it. No names, no emails, no profiles.

Why this experiment matters

Most emotions remain invisible. Yet, at any moment, millions of people are experiencing something intense, quiet, joyful, heavy, or uncertain. Seeing the collective mood can help us zoom out: your feeling is real, and it’s also part of a larger human landscape.

Over time, the “emotional weather” changes. Observing those shifts can reveal patterns: contrast between regions, time zones, and moments. The map becomes a living snapshot of diversity—without judging it.

The 10,000 participants milestone

Mood2Know aims for a simple threshold: 10,000 participants. The more people contribute, the more meaningful the collective picture becomes. With enough responses, the map becomes less “random dots” and more like a real signal.

FAQ

Is Mood2Know a diagnostic tool?

No. Mood2Know does not analyze or diagnose. It is a minimalist participation space—nothing more.

Do I need an account?

No. You can participate without signing up. The experience is intentionally frictionless.

Why do you ask for my location?

The map needs a location to place your mood. The goal is to show a collective picture across places.

Can I change my mood later?

Yes. You can submit again later—your newest mood replaces your previous one for your anonymous ID.

How can I help?

Participate, share the map, and invite one person. The experiment becomes meaningful with volume.
